History of the Building

Ninety years of entertainment. Ready for its next chapter.

6230 W Sunset Blvd has held some of the most significant moments in Hollywood history. Now God is writing the next chapter, and the harvest is the story.

1938
Earl Carroll Theatre

A 1,000-seat “entertainment palace” with a 60-foot revolving stage opens Dec 26, 1938. Walter Winchell dubs it the “Most Beautiful Girls in the World” theatre.

1950s – 60s
Moulin Rouge & Aquarius

Nightclub hosting Frank Sinatra, Ella Fitzgerald, and Nat King Cole. Later home of the rock musical Hair in Hollywood.

1997 – 2020
Nickelodeon on Sunset

West Coast production hub for Nickelodeon. Taping of All That, Kenan & Kel, The Amanda Show, iCarly, Big Time Rush, and Victorious.

2022 – Now
Restoration & For Sale

Art Deco marquee and neon restored. Designated a Historic-Cultural Monument. Listed at $12.75M. The door opens for God’s house.

Sources: LA Historic Theatre Foundation  •  LA Conservancy  •  Wikimedia Commons

A City God Keeps Returning To

Four Waves of Revival in LA.

Each one shaped the global Church. Each one moved within miles of 6230 W Sunset Blvd. We believe God is writing another chapter, and this house sits right in the middle of it.

1906
Azusa Street Revival
3.8 Miles from 6230 Sunset

Led by William J. Seymour at 312 Azusa St. Three years of continuous revival. Crowds of 300 to 1,500 daily. Birthplace of global Pentecostalism, now 600M+ adherents worldwide.

1923
Aimee Semple McPherson • Angelus Temple
3.1 Miles from 6230 Sunset

America’s first megachurch opens in Echo Park. 5,300-seat auditorium, “continuous revival.” Birth of the Foursquare Church. Weekly attendance reached 10,000 in the 1920s.

1930s
Henrietta Mears • Hollywood First Pres.
0.4 Miles from 6230 Sunset

Grew the Sunday School from 450 to 6,500. Discipled Billy Graham, Bill Bright (Campus Crusade / CRU), and 400+ who entered ministry. Hollywood Christian Group reached TV and film stars.

1967
The Jesus People Movement
LA / SoCal Epicenter

Hippie revival born from LA’s counterculture. Chuck Smith’s Calvary Chapel baptized hundreds monthly. The Hollywood Free Paper. Gave birth to modern worship music and contemporary Christian culture.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions.

What is Harvest Hands?
Harvest Hands is a $3,000,000 building campaign to secure and prepare a permanent home for Fearless Church. It is a faith-fueled, two-phase initiative: Phase One ($1.5M) covers possessing the building, and Phase Two ($1.5M) covers preparing it for ministry.
When is the Phase One giving window?
Phase One runs for 5 Sundays: Sunday, April 26 through Pentecost Sunday, May 24. Our Phase One goal is $1.5M, and we are believing God to possess the promise in this window.
What are the two ways to give?
There are two separate, distinct ways to say yes, and you can do one or both.

1. A one-time gift. This is actual giving, not a pledge or an IOU. Real dollars move into the campaign the moment you give.

2. A 12-month faith commitment. This is ongoing monthly giving across a full year. You tell God what you are believing Him for, and you give toward it month by month.
How long is the faith commitment?
The faith commitment is a 12-month window. You can fulfill it weekly, monthly, or in lump sums based on what works for your family.
Is my pledge in addition to my regular tithe?
Yes. Harvest Hands is intended to be a sacrificial offering above and beyond your regular tithes and offerings, so the day-to-day ministry of Fearless can keep moving forward while we build for the future.
What if my financial situation changes?
Your pledge is a faith commitment, not a contract. If your situation changes, simply reach out to our team and we will adjust together. We trust God and we trust you.
Are gifts tax deductible?
Yes. Fearless Church is a registered 501(c)(3), and all gifts to Harvest Hands are tax deductible to the fullest extent allowed by law. You will receive a year-end giving statement.
Where exactly does my gift go?
One hundred percent of every gift designated to Harvest Hands goes directly to the building campaign. None of it covers operational expenses or staff salaries.
What happens if we don’t raise enough to secure this specific building?
We are praying this is the building God has for us, but if we don’t reach our Phase One goal in time, every dollar contributed will still go toward getting Fearless a home.
Can I give from stock, an IRA, or my estate?
Absolutely. We welcome creative giving including appreciated stock, IRA qualified charitable distributions, donor-advised funds, real estate, and planned/estate gifts. Click the Give button in the Creative Giving section above and our team will walk you through every step.
How do I stay updated on what God is doing?
We will share regular updates by email and from the platform on Sundays. Make sure you are on the Fearless email list and following @fearless_la so you do not miss a single milestone.
